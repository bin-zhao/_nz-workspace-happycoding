Compliant. Following rules, orders, or requests; ready to agree with others.
Essay. A short piece of writing on a particular subject, often expressing an individual's opinion or analysis.
Coherence. The quality of being logical, consistent, and easy to understand; when all parts of something fit together well.
Cohesion. The act or state of sticking together firmly; the natural or logical connection of the parts of a whole. Often used in writing to refer to how sentences and paragraphs link together.
Idiomatic. Using or related to idioms; natural-sounding to a native speaker. An idiom is a phrase whose meaning cannot be understood from the ordinary meaning of its words (e.g., "kick the bucket").
Podcast. A digital audio file available on the internet for downloading to a computer or mobile device, typically available as a series.
Comprehension. The ability to understand something; the act or process of understanding.
Transcript. A written, typed, or printed copy of words that have been spoken; an official record of a student's grades.
Propensity. A natural tendency or inclination to behave in a particular way.
Summarize. To give a brief statement of the main points of something.
Accents. Distinctive modes of pronunciation of a language, especially one associated with a particular nation, locality, or social class.
Intonation. The rise and fall of the voice in speaking, which can convey meaning or emotion.
Concisely. In a brief but comprehensive way; in a way that avoids unnecessary words.
Tendency. An inclination toward a particular characteristic or type of behavior.
Inclination. A person's natural preference or tendency to act or feel a particular way; a disposition.
