Idiomatic. Using or related to idioms; natural-sounding to a native speaker. An idiom is a phrase whose meaning cannot be understood from the ordinary meaning of its words (e.g., "kick the bucket").

Accents. Distinctive modes of pronunciation of a language, especially one associated with a particular nation, locality, or social class.
Intonation. The rise and fall of the voice in speaking, which can convey meaning or emotion.
Concisely. In a brief but comprehensive way; in a way that avoids unnecessary words.
Tendency. An inclination toward a particular characteristic or type of behavior.
Inclination. A person's natural preference or tendency to act or feel a particular way; a disposition.
Exaggeration. A statement that represents something as better or worse than it really is.
Entertaining. Providing amusement or enjoyment.
Impaired. Weakened or damaged; functioning poorly or less effectively.

Genres. Categories of artistic composition, characterized by similarities in form, style, or subject matter.
Crime. An action or omission that constitutes an offense that may be prosecuted by the state and is punishable by law.
Comedy. Professional entertainment consisting of jokes and satirical sketches, intended to make an audience laugh.
Compelling. Evoking interest, attention, or admiration in a powerfully irresistible way; not able to be refuted.
Procedures. An established or official way of doing something.
Interrogative. Having the form of a question; asking a question.
Architect. A person who designs buildings and in many cases also supervises their construction.
Dictate. To say words aloud to be typed, written down, or recorded; to lay down authoritatively.
Dictation. The action of saying words aloud to be typed, written down, or recorded; the act of giving orders or imposing terms.
Vacuum. A space entirely devoid of matter; a device that cleans floors and other surfaces by suction.
Janitor. A person employed to look after a public building; a caretaker.
Hockey. A game played on an ice rink or field between two teams of players who use curved sticks to drive a small ball or puck into the opponent's goal.
Puck. A black disc made of hard rubber, used in ice hockey.
Hazardous. Risky; dangerous.
Intensive. Concentrated on a single area or subject or into a short time; characterized by a high degree of effort or concentration.
Extensive. Covering or affecting a large area; large in amount or scale.
Fatigue. Extreme tiredness, typically resulting from mental or physical exertion or illness.
Nutrients. Substances that provide nourishment essential for the maintenance of life and for growth.
Campus. The grounds and buildings of a university, college, or school.
Satirical. Using humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize people's stupidity or vices.
Estate. A large area of land, usually with a large house, belonging to one person; or all the money and property owned by a particular person, especially at death.
Distractor. Something that draws one's attention away from what they are doing or should be doing.
Exogenous. Originating from outside; caused by external factors.
Endogenous. Originating from within; caused by internal factors.
Sinusoidal. Having the form of a sine wave; characterized by a smooth, repetitive oscillation.
Rhythm. A strong, regular, repeated pattern of movement or sound.
Edamame. Young soybeans, typically served in the pod, often steamed or boiled and lightly salted.
Soybean. A type of bean grown mainly in China and Japan, used to produce oil, flour, and animal feed.
Boil. (Of a liquid) reach the temperature at which it bubbles and turns to vapor; to cook (food) by boiling it.
Flavor. The distinctive taste of a food or drink.
Soy sauce. A strong-tasting, dark brown liquid made from fermented soya beans and used especially in Chinese and Japanese cooking.
Vinegar. A sour-tasting liquid containing acetic acid, obtained by fermenting alcohol, used as a condiment or for pickling.
Intoxication. The state of being poisoned (especially by alcohol or drugs); extreme excitement or exhilaration.
Anxious. Experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness, typically about an event or something with an uncertain outcome; wanting something very much.
Excruciating. Intensely painful; mentally agonizing.
Vulnerability. The quality or state of being exposed to the possibility of being attacked or harmed, either physically or emotionally.
Associate. To **associate** means to connect someone or something with something else in your mind, or to spend time with someone as a friend or colleague. As a noun, an **associate** is a partner or colleague, or a person with limited membership in a group.
Community. A **community** is a group of people living in the same place or having a particular characteristic in common. It can also refer to the feeling of fellowship with others as a result of sharing common attitudes, interests, and goals.
Cultural. **Cultural** describes things related to the customs, arts, social institutions, and achievements of a particular nation, people, or group. It often involves shared beliefs and practices.
Economic. **Economic** refers to things relating to the economy, or the system of how money, industry, and trade are organized in a country or region. It deals with the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services.
Individual. An **individual** is a single person, distinct from a group or collective. It can also describe something belonging to or characteristic of a particular person or thing.
Education. The process of teaching or learning, especially in a school or college, or the knowledge that you get from this.
Organization. An **organization** is an organized body of people with a particular purpose, such as a business, government department, or charity. It refers to the structure and arrangement of elements working together.
Particular. **Particular** refers to one specific thing or person, rather than a general one. It can also mean unusual or special.
Policy. A **policy** is a course or principle of action adopted or proposed by a government, party, business, or individual. It acts as a guide for decisions and actions.
Population. A **population** is all the inhabitants of a particular town, area, or country. It can also refer to a specific group of animals or objects in a given area.
Practice. **Practice** refers to the actual application or use of an idea, method, or belief, as opposed to theories about it. It can also mean custom, habit, or repeated exercise to improve a skill.
Process. A **process** is a series of actions or steps taken in order to achieve a particular end. It describes how something is done or how something happens.
Region. A **region** is an area, especially part of a country or the world, having definable characteristics but not always fixed boundaries. It is often characterized by climate, geography, or culture.
Significant. **Significant** means sufficiently great or important to be worthy of attention; noteworthy. It indicates that something has a great deal of meaning or influence.
Social. **Social** relates to society or its organization. It involves the interaction of individuals and groups within a community.
Strategy. A **strategy** is a plan of action or policy designed to achieve a major or overall aim. It involves careful planning and decision-making to reach a goal.
Theory. A **theory** is a system of ideas intended to explain something, especially one based on general principles independent of the thing to be explained. In science, it's a well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world, based on a body of facts that have been repeatedly confirmed through observation and experiment.
Treatment. **Treatment** refers to the manner in which someone or something is handled or dealt with. In medicine, it is the use of a particular substance or method to cure or improve a health problem.
Appropriate. **Appropriate** means suitable or proper in the circumstances. It implies that something is right or fitting for a particular situation or purpose.
Argue. To **argue** means to give reasons or evidence in support of an idea, action, or theory, typically with the aim of persuading others. It can also mean to dispute or disagree, often in a forceful way.
Assess. To **assess** means to evaluate or estimate the nature, ability, or quality of something. It involves judging or determining the value, significance, or extent of something.
Assessment. An **assessment** is the evaluation or estimation of the nature, quality, or ability of someone or something. It is the process of appraising or judging.
Basis. The **basis** is the fundamental reason, principle, or cause for something. It is the foundation or starting point upon which something is constructed or reasoned.
Benefit. A **benefit** is an advantage or profit gained from something. As a verb, to **benefit** means to receive an advantage or to be useful to someone or something.
Category. A **category** is a class or division of people or things regarded as having particular shared characteristics. It is a group into which things are classified.
Characteristic. A **characteristic** is a feature or quality belonging typically to a person, place, or thing and serving to identify it. It is a distinguishing trait.
Concept. A **concept** is an abstract idea; a general notion. It is a general principle or understanding of something.
Demand. **Demand** refers to the insistent and peremptory request or desire for something. In economics, it is the quantity of a good or service that consumers are willing and able to purchase at various prices. As a verb, to **demand** means to ask for something forcefully.
Determine. To **determine** means to establish or ascertain exactly, typically as a result of research or calculation. It can also mean to decide firmly, or to be a strong factor in deciding an outcome.
Distribution. **Distribution** is the action of sharing something out among a number of recipients. It also refers to the way in which something is spread or arranged over an area.
Evidence. **Evidence** is the available body of facts or information indicating whether a belief or proposition is true or valid. It serves as proof or support for an argument.
Examine. To **examine** means to inspect (someone or something) in detail to determine their nature, condition, or qualities. It often implies a careful and systematic investigation.
Extent. **Extent** refers to the degree to which something is or is believed to be the case, or the area covered by something. It indicates the scope or range of an effect or influence.
Identity. **Identity** is the fact of being who or what a person or thing is. It refers to the distinguishing characteristics or qualities of an individual or group.
Impact. **Impact** means the strong effect or influence that something has on a situation or person. As a verb, to **impact** means to have a strong effect on.
Indicate. To **indicate** means to point out or show something. It can also mean to suggest or be a sign of something.
Intervention. **Intervention** is the action or process of intervening, which means coming between two things, or becoming involved in a situation to alter or prevent an outcome. It implies deliberate action to influence an event.
Mechanism. A **mechanism** is a system of parts working together in a machine; a piece of machinery. More broadly, it is a natural or established process by which something takes place or is brought about.
Nature. **Nature** refers to the phenomena of the physical world collectively, including plants, animals, the landscape, and other features and products of the earth. It can also mean the inherent character or qualities of a person or thing.
Observe. To **observe** means to watch carefully the way something happens or the way someone does something, especially in order to learn more about it. It can also mean to notice or perceive, or to obey a rule.
Participant. A **participant** is a person who takes part in something. It refers to someone involved in an activity, event, or study.
Pattern. A **pattern** is a regular or comprehensible arrangement or sequence. It refers to a recurring form or design, or a model to be followed.
Percent. **Percent** (or percent) means by a specified amount in every hundred. It is a proportion out of a hundred.
Perspective. **Perspective** is a particular attitude toward or way of regarding something; a point of view. It refers to a way of looking at or understanding things.
Physical. **Physical** relates to the body as opposed to the mind. It also refers to things relating to matter and energy, or the laws of nature.
Potential. **Potential** refers to qualities or abilities that may be developed and lead to future success or usefulness. It means having the capacity to become or develop into something in the future.
Pressure. **Pressure** is the continuous physical force exerted on or against an object by something in contact with it. It can also mean the use of persuasion, influence, or intimidation to make someone do something.
Primary. **Primary** means of chief importance; principal. It refers to something that is first in order, rank, or importance.

Procedure. A **procedure** is an established or official way of doing something. It is a set of actions that is the official or accepted way of doing something.
Production. **Production** is the action of making or manufacturing from components or raw materials, or the process of growing or supplying something. It can also refer to the total amount produced.
Programme (bre) / program (ame). A **programme** (or **program**) is a planned series of future events, performances, or broadcasts. In computing, it's a set of instructions enabling a computer to perform a task. It is a set of related measures or activities with a particular long-term aim.
Relevant. **Relevant** means closely connected or appropriate to what is being done or considered. It indicates that something is pertinent and significant to the current topic or situation.

Sequence. A **sequence** is a particular order in which related things follow each other. It is a series of things that follow one another in a logical or chronological order.
Similar. **Similar** means resembling without being identical. It indicates that two or more things share qualities or characteristics but are not exactly the same.
Site. A **site** is an area of ground on which a town, building, or monument is constructed or situated. It can also refer to a location where something significant is located or occurring, or a website.
Technology. **Technology** is the application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes, especially in industry. It refers to machinery and equipment developed from the application of scientific knowledge.
Variation. **Variation** is a change or difference in condition, amount, or level, typically with certain limits. It indicates diversity or a slight difference from a standard or norm.
Whereas. **Whereas** is a conjunction used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or contradicts a previous statement, or to indicate a difference between two facts or situations. It means "in contrast" or "while on the contrary."
